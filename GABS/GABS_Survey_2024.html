2025 GABS Graduate Experience Survey

In November of 2024, GABS aimed to gauge the Biology graduate student experience through an anonymous survey.

The survey assessed (1) Financial Support & Well-being, (2) Event Satisfaction (3) Perception of Departmental, PI, or Teaching Support, (4) General Feeling of Safety (Emotional/Physical), through a combination of multiple-choice and open-ended questions.

The survey was disseminated to 77 graduate students and we received 39 complete responses for a response rate of just over 50%.

Demographics of Respondents

82% (32/39) of respondents were 25-34 years old.

92% (36/39) of respondents are completing their PhD and 8% are completing their Masters. Of the 39 respondents, 46% (18/39) are in the MCDB program and 54% (21/39) are in the EEB program.

The largest cohort represented in the survey was the 2022 cohort (2nd years) who made up ~23% (9/39) of respondents.

About half (19/39) of respondents hold a masters or transferred from another graduate program while about half (20/39) hold no prior graduate school experience.

~24% (9/37) of respondents identified as belonging underrepresented group and that split was evenly represented in EEB and MCDB programs.

~23% (9/39) of respondents were international students with ~89% (8/9) of those respondents belonging to the MCDB program.
